üßÆ Class 1: Division Algorithm & Primes

We discussed the Division Algorithm:

$$ \text{For integers } a, b \text{ (with } b \ne 0), \exists \text{ unique integers } q, r \text{ such that } a = bq + r, \quad 0 \le r < |b| $$

Definition of prime numbers: A number greater than 1 with no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.

Exercise: Prove that if a prime \( p \mid ab \), then \( p \mid a \) or \( p \mid b \).

üìè Class 2: GCD, Euclidean Algorithm

Definition of GCD: The greatest common divisor of two integers \( a \) and \( b \), not both zero, is the largest positive integer \( d \) such that \( d \mid a \) and \( d \mid b \).

Euclidean Algorithm:

$$ \gcd(a, b) = \gcd(b, r) \text{ where } a = bq + r $$ Repeat until remainder is 0.
